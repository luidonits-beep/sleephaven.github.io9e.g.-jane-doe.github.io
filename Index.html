THE IMPACT OF SLEEP DEPRIVATION ON MENTAL HEALTH AND ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE

Overview

Sleep deprivation is simply not getting enough sleep for your body to work well. Imagine your body is a phone, and sleep is how you charge it. When you're sleep-deprived, you're running on a low battery all day.

This can happen for simple reasons, like staying up late to watch a movie or study for a test.

When you don't get enough sleep, you might feel grumpy or have a hard time thinking clearly. You might even catch more colds. The good news is that you can fix it by getting a good night's rest. It’s the easiest way to feel great and have more energy!

FAQs

Sleep deprivation occurs when an individual doesn't get enough sleep or has poor quality sleep, leading to physical and mental fatigue.

Symptoms include fatigue, difficulty concentrating, mood swings, irritability, and decreased productivity.

It can contribute to anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues.

Yes, sleep deprivation can be treated with lifestyle changes, sleep therapy, and professional help when needed.
